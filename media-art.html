New Media Art

Exploring the limitations, characteristics and impact of media technology

Chanwoo Lee is an HCI researcher, design engineer and media artist based in London and Seoul.

He designs and develops computer interaction, interface and system for human creativity & productivity. Defining relationship between human and data, he designs information environment.

He is currently pursuing MA/MSc in Innovation Design Engineering at Imperial College London and Royal College of Art.

Snap AR Experience Research Project

Shared playful experience of non-verbal communication with human body on Spectacles and WebAR

Display, whether it's transparent or not, right in front of your eyes might be quite lonely. There's some subtle information and message in non-verbal communication and it's often lost within AR interaction. Using non-linear gamified storytelling and body movement leveraging Connected Lenses, and Hand Tracking.

  • HCI Design Engineer

  • Team Yuebin Zhang(Gamification, UX), Nahuel Basterretche(Render Pipeline, Development), Chun Guo(storytelling, 3D Art), Jake Buckley (3D Art)

Sunny Day

Pushing the boundaries of creative expression with cutting-edge XR technology.

Solarpunk media art about a future where pixels approach the realm of atom, and the climate crisis has been overcome.

Interactive MR system for immersive storytelling capable of high degree of freedom in creative expressions, integrating UWB sensors, Azure Kinect and htc Vive.

As a research team lead, I led research, design & engineering of the MR system and developed wearable input device.

As a co-artist, we worked on research for future scenario and current humanity's climate technologies.
