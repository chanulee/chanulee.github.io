The Computer Architect Manifesto: Back to Human

Computer designers and engineers are the new architects of the information age.

Throughout history, architects have designed and engineered environments and ‘machines for living in’ throughout human history.
Today, as computer architects, we redefine these spaces—places both virtual and cognitive.

Computer is a machine that we live in and with.
It is an entity in itself and an environment at the same time.
Data becomes information when interpreted by humans,
and we design that relationship around computers.

It serves and affects human -

human behaviour,
thinking,
cognition,
sense,
habits,
relationships,
playing,
learning,
working,
loving,
and living.

"Making better things" does not inherently mean "making things better."
We must reconsider humanism in the information age, in our era of relentless technology advancement.

We stand at a pivotal moment:
What kind of life do humans envision?
How can we make it happen with computers?
How do we ensure it reflects human values?
